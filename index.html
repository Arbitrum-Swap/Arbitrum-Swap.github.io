How to Do an Arbitrum Swap

To complete an arbitrum swap, connect a Web3 wallet like MetaMask to an Arbitrum decentralized exchange, select the tokens you want to trade, set slippage tolerance, confirm the transaction, and approve it in your wallet. Most swaps on Arbitrum One settle within seconds and cost under a dollar in gas.

Arbitrum One is a Layer 2 rollup built by Offchain Labs, designed to reduce Ethereum gas fees while inheriting its security model (https://developer.arbitrum.io/).

Follow these steps. Do them in order. You’ll be done in two minutes.

1. Add Arbitrum Network

Open MetaMask and add Arbitrum One via official RPC settings or the built-in network list. Ensure you’re not on Ethereum mainnet before proceeding.

2. Fund Your Wallet

Bridge ETH or stablecoins to Arbitrum using a supported bridge. You need a small amount of ETH for Arbitrum gas fees.

3. Choose a DEX

Select Uniswap Arbitrum, SushiSwap Arbitrum, or Camelot DEX. Check liquidity before confirming.

4. Set Slippage

Adjust slippage tolerance (commonly 0.1%–0.5% for stable pairs, higher for volatile tokens).

5. Confirm Swap

Review output amount, fees, and price impact. Approve token spending if required, then confirm the swap.

Choosing Tokens for Arbitrum Swap

Popular pairs include ARB token swap routes, ETH/USDC, and stablecoin trades. High liquidity pools reduce slippage and price impact, especially on larger orders.

Managing Slippage Tolerance

Low slippage protects you from price swings, but too low can cause transaction failure. For volatile tokens, increase tolerance slightly to avoid repeated rejections.

Confirming on MetaMask

After clicking swap, MetaMask displays gas cost and contract details. Double-check the contract address and total cost before approving the arbitrum swap.

Arbitrum Swap and Liquidity Pools

An arbitrum swap relies on automated market maker liquidity pools that hold token pairs and determine prices algorithmically. Deeper pools reduce slippage and stabilize execution, while shallow pools increase volatility and trading costs.

How Pools Price Arbitrum Swap Trades
